How to Choose the Right Kitchen Utensils

The kitchen is where creative processes flourish. Be it the recipe that was passed on from generation to generation or something new that you tried, it is all part of a creative process that originates from the heart and out of love.This makes your kitchen the most important part of the home.And as such your kitchen should be equipped with the best of the tools to ensure that your artistic process is not interrupted.There are essential tools and then there are tools that you do not probably need but really want. It is very easy to get carried away when buying kitchen equipment. Just keep in mind the following points before choosing your kitchen utensils and you are sure not to go overboard. Do You Need it?

To answer this question, tell us if you have a good-sized kitchen or one of those tiny apartment kitchens. A good-sized kitchen gives you the creative freedom to pick whatever you want. But if you have a small kitchen, you might want to give a thought and choose only what you would use. Spatulas, spoons (stirring and serving), ladles, wire whisk, grater, peeler and measuring cups are an absolute must. On the other hand, you also have a pizza cutter, zester, masher, etc which can be optional. When it comes to pots and pans, kadai of various sizes, frying pans and tawas are an absolute must. THEIR USES 1. Tongs Tongs deserve the top spot on this list because it is one of the most useful kitchen utensils. In a nutshell, it is a kitchen utensil that is used to hold things, in cooking and serving food. Technically speaking, cooks use tongs to grip or hold food items. In the kitchen, food items often become too hot or too cold. As a result, you cannot hold them with your bare hands. Tongs solve this problem. By gripping extremely hot or cold food items, you donâ€™t have to put your precious hands at risk. In addition, you can use tongs to handle food that you do not want to handle with your hands. These include breading, sticky food, and mixing greens. 2. Slotted Spoon As its name suggests, a slotted spoon is a large kitchen utensil with slots or holes. With a long handle and properly positioned holes, the slotted spoon works to keep your hands away from food items with extreme temperatures. Slotted spoons come in several materials, shapes, and sizes. To choose a slotted spoon that best suits your needs, you must consider the type of food you frequently prepare. You must also consider the size of the holes and the heat resistance of the materials. A slotted spoon becomes extremely useful when preparing soups, pasta, stew, deep-fried items, and the like. 3. Large Spoon Speaking of spoons, you must consider getting another spoon. This time, you must consider getting a large spoon, A large spoon can be used to do a lot of things. These include stirring, mixing, serving, and transferring food.

Is it of Good Quality?

How can you differentiate between an off the rack cheap vessel or utensil and a quality product? It is not quite easy unless you go for branded products like Prestige or Wonderchef. A high-quality product would go for a longer period of time without any need for replacement or repairs. A quality vessel distributes heat evenly and doesn't scratch easily; A good spoon and ladle would not disturb the coating on your vessel. Invest in a quality product that stays with you for years and throughout your cooking adventure.

Does it Exceed Your Budget?

As with every purchase, plan your budget before jumping the gun. It is not wise to stock your kitchen at the expense of a hole in your purse. Go for the kitchen essentials that you simply cannot do without. As for the rest, reach for cheaper products until you can pitch in for quality products. Another tip when on a tight budget is to go for individual products and not a set. Sets can be pretty expensive, but with individual products, you get to choose your most essential needs.
